EffuseNet: Deep Learning for Differentiating Between Exudative and Transudative Pleural Effusions Through Ultrasounds

Posters
Abstract

Pleural effusion, an abnormal collection of fluid in the pleural space, is the most common presentation of pulmonary disease with over 1.5 million cases identified per year in the United States alone. Patients presenting with symptoms suspicious for an effusion often undergo diagnostic chest radiographs to identify the presence of an effusion. However, these radiologic studies cannot definitively diagnose whether the effusion is a transudate or an exudate. The only current method known to accurately differentiate the type of effusion present is to perform a thoracentesis–an invasive procedure where the excess fluid is drained and then analyzed using Light’s criteria. However, a thoracentesis is an inefficient and expensive procedure that may not be readily available and can further debilitate critically ill patients, often compromising or delaying treatment. Conversely, thoracic sonography, a non-invasive modality, has traditionally been used to diagnose the presence of a pleural effusion, but radiologists are unable to use an ultrasound to accurately identify the type of effusion. This dilemma prompted my current research question: is there a predictable and reliable method to differentiate between transudative and exudative pleural effusions based solely on ultrasound images? To this end, I developed EffuseNet, a program that uses deep convolutional neural networks trained on ultrasound images and associated labels to non-invasively differentiate between transudative and exudative pleural effusions. Several models and methods were experimentally performed including convolutional neural networks and transfer learning. The models used in the experiment were then tested on test data and evaluated using several different metrics such as a confusion matrix and classification accuracy. Although my program is novel in its application, the results were statistically significant. With such widespread prevalence of pleural effusions diagnosed annually, EffuseNet, the first of its kind, is revolutionary and adds a significant benefit to society.
