In April-May of 2021, I volunteered as a member of the teaching team for Code In Place. This online course was offered by Stanford University during the COVID-19 pandemic. It brought together 12,000 students and 1100 volunteer teachers participating from around the world. The course is a 6-week introduction to Python programming using materials from the first half of Stanford‚Äôs CS 106A course. As a volunteer section leader, I prepared and taught a weekly discussion section of 8-10 students to supplement professors' lectures.

This was my first time formally teaching and I had a fun time! I definately learned a ton from the experience and found that I enjoyed explaining things. More importantly however, watching my students grow as programmers was more rewarding.

As this was an introductory course, I wanted to show my students the powers of programming and really how beautiful computer science really is.

The contents covered in the course were:

  1. Welcome to Karel
  2. Control Flow
  3. Decompostionl
  4. Intro to Python
  5. Expressions
  6. Control Flow Revisited
  7. Functions Revisited
  8. Core Complete
  9. Images (my favorite!)
  10. Lists
  11. Text
  12. Dictionaries
  13. Files
  14. Data Science
